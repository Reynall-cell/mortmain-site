Short pitch

Squalor is a multiplayer extraction game set in a medieval kingdom devoured by plague. Players enter dangerous zones, scavenge what they can, and try to make it back to safety with their lives and their loot intact. Combat is first-person, directional, and built on stance, parry, and feint — the duel is a conversation written in steel. Loot is hardcore: die in a raid and you may lose everything you carried. The world is alive with bandits, undead, beasts of folklore, and other players who want what you carry.

Squalor is heavily inspired by the combat of Kingdom Come: Deliverance and For Honor, the extraction loop of Escape from Tarkov, and the dark folklore of The Witcher. The studio's central interest is in worlds that remember — and in players who must outlive that memory.

Key features

  • Four-direction melee combat with stance switching, parries, feints, and combos.
  • Hardcore extraction loop with persistent stash and per-raid loot risk.
  • An insurance system that includes a "find your killer" mechanic — coin recovered through revenge, not magic.
  • A living world of AI bandits, undead, and beasts of folklore, alongside other players.
  • Skill progression based on technique unlocks, not stat inflation. Knowledge is power; numbers are not.
  • A pantheon and bestiary rooted in a deliberate, layered lore — what is shown, what is hidden, what is forgotten on purpose.
  • Proximity voice. Friendly fire. Solo or in squads up to four.

About the studio

Mortmain Studio is an independent workshop building dark fantasy games. We do not give out names. We work in the dark on purpose — not because we hide, but because the work matters more than the worker. Squalor is our first game.
