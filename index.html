Capitulum I
— The Premise —

The kingdom rots. The fields are done. Those who stayed are those who can bear the cold, the blade, the plague. Take what you can carry home. Leave the rest where it has fallen. One will come, when you are gone — takes your gold. Takes you alone.

Do not breathe upon the door. Do not gather what is poor. Do not count the crows in flight — The third crow takes the light.

— from the Sermons of the Last Bishop

Capitulum II
— What Awaits —

Four pillars of the dying world

II Carry It or Lose It

Capitulum · I

Directional Combat

Four directions of the strike. Stance work. Parries take timing, feints take nerve. A duel is a conversation in steel. Learn to hear it before it hears you.

Capitulum · II

Carry It or Lose It

Make it back with your spoils, or die beneath them. Insure what you can stand to lose. For the rest, go and take it back yourself. The road forgives no debt.

Capitulum · III

A World That Bites Back

Bandits at the roadside. Undead in the crypts. Beasts in the deep wood. Every enemy speaks the same language of war as you — there are no light paths here, only sharper steel.

Capitulum · IV

No Magic. No Mercy.

No fireballs. No second tries. Your skill is your sorcery. Your armour is your prayer. And strength runs out before the sun goes down.

Steel is honest. The man holding it, less so.

— old saying of the road

Capitulum III
— The Road Book —

Who walks the road

Twenty lives before the plague. Choose who you were — it decides how you survive, what you owe, and what you hear in the dark. No gift makes you stronger against another man.

An early design. Gifts and burdens are named in words, not numbers — the numbers will come with the game, and will change more than once.

Colophon
— The Workshop —

Mortmain Studio

Mortmain is a small workshop devoted to games at the edge of medieval imagination. We are interested in what survives when the world doesn't. Squalor is our first dispatch from the dark.

A.D. MMXXVI
— From the elder folio —

The Pantheon

Six were once named openly. One was named to silence the others. None of the seven answer when called.

✠
☉
The Reaper
Sister of many mouths
Once she was given milk and the first cut furrow. They called her Golden Mother. Now her gifts arrive unbidden — the wound that will not close, the grain that ripens past its season, the child grown wrong in the womb.
Of harvest, of healing, of all that grows past its bound.
⚒
The Old Smith
Father of the unbreakable word
He hammered the first crown and the first chain on the same anvil. Oaths sworn to him cannot be unsworn. Kings forgot this. The oath did not.
Of craft, of pact, of war that does not end.
☽
The Whisperer
Mistress behind the veil
She is the dream you remember on the wrong morning. She is the name you almost spoke. The mad hear her clearly. The wise do not pretend to.
Of dream, of secret, of the thin place between knowing and forgetting.
❄
The Pale Sister
She who breathes the snow
The plague hates her cold. So do the cathedrals. Villages still leave her gifts of ice in their wells — when no priest is watching. They say the winters are growing longer. They do not say why.
Of winter, of clarity, of the long sleep that keeps worse things sleeping.
🜂
The Hearth-Keeper
He who warms the door
His was the fire that kept the wolf out. Now his fire is in every torch that finds a heretic. He does not understand what they have made of him. He still tries to keep them warm.
Of hearth, of light, of the pyre that no longer asks who burns.
⚱
The Name-Keeper
Scribe of the last page
He writes every name in ash. He has not stopped writing for two centuries. Some names he can no longer find. Those names walk the roads in his place, looking.
Of ending, of memory, of the small mercy of being known.
◇
The Pale Sovereign
The faceless one
In the year of the first mourning the cathedrals were rebuilt for him. The bishops swore he had always been. The crown agreed. No icon shows his face. No prayer he answers is recorded. He is the only god whose worship was a decree.
Of decree, of replacement, of the silence where six older voices used to sing.
— other names exist. they have been struck from the folio. they will return when struck pages do —
— From a hunter's marginalia —

The Bestiary

What walks the roads after dark. Carry steel. Carry salt. Carry the names of your dead.

⚜
𓂀
Strzyga
— Cursed, woman-born —
She was once a daughter of some house. Now she eats marrow. Sunlight slows her. Iron at the threshold deters her. Speaking her name aloud is said to wake her. I do not test this.
☠
The Newly Risen
— Undead, freshly turned —
Plague-dead who were not given proper rite. They are hungry, not angry. They remember the road home and try to walk it. Strike the skull. Burn what remains.
⚰
The Forgotten
— Undead, nameless —
Older than the newly risen. Their names have left the world entirely. They search. If you knew the name they once had, they would weep and lie down. Few do.
⚔
Armoured Sleepers
— Undead, sworn —
Skeletons in the panoply of old orders. They do not rest because some oath was not fulfilled. They will not say which. They fight as well as they did living. They tire less.
𓂺
Drowners
— Of still water —
First mourning casualties, drowned in the early panic. They cling to riverbanks. They drag the unwary down into water that does not always have a bottom.
🜨
The Leshen
— Of the deep wood —
Wardens of altars now overgrown. They do not hunt. They punish trespass. Cut no living wood in their groves. Leave them honey. Do not look back.
𖠰
Wyvern
— Of the high places —
Older than the first mourning. Older than the kingdoms. They do not serve anyone. They eat livestock when the winters are bad. The winters are bad now.
⚒
Brigands
— Living, desperate —
The worst sort of monster, in that they reason. They were farmers, soldiers, sons. Hunger made them this. Some of them remember kindness. Most do not.
𖠰
Boar
— Living, beastly —
A boar. Nothing more. Not every danger in this world is cursed. Some are simply what they are. The boar does not care for our wars.
— other things walk the wood. I have not written of them. I did not survive to write of them. —
